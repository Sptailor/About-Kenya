Capital & Major cities
The capital city of Kenya is Nairobi. Other major cities include Mombasa (a coastal city and major port), Kisumu, and Nakuru.

Landforms
The Great Rift Valley runs through Kenya from north to south, influencing the country's topography. Mount Kenya, an extinct stratovolcano and the highest mountain in Kenya, is located in the central part of the country. Other notable features include the Aberdare Range, the Mau Escarpment, and the Nyika Plateau.
Lakes and Rivers
Kenya is home to several lakes, including Lake Victoria (shared with Uganda and Tanzania), Lake Turkana (the world's largest desert lake), and Lake Naivasha. Major rivers include the Tana River, Athi-Galana-Sabaki River, and Ewaso Ng'iro.
Wildlife and Conservation
Kenya is renowned for its diverse and abundant wildlife. The country has numerous national parks and reserves, such as the Maasai Mara National Reserve and Amboseli National Park. The Great Migration, where large herds of wildebeest and zebras move between Tanzania and Kenya, is a spectacular natural event that occurs annually in the Maasai Mara.
Climate
Kenya has a diverse climate due to its varying topography and geography. The country can be divided into several climatic zones, including coastal, central highlands, eastern, and northern regions. The country is situated near the equator, and its climate ranges from tropical along the coast to arid in the interior.
